📈 What is BMI and How is it Calculated?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a simple screening tool that estimates body fat based on your height and weight. It was developed by Belgian mathematician Adolphe Quetelet in the 1830s and is still used worldwide by doctors and health organizations.

BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height (m)2

Step-by-Step Example

For a person weighing 70 kg with a height of 175 cm:

  1. Convert height to meters: 175 cm = 1.75 m
  2. Square the height: 1.75 × 1.75 = 3.0625
  3. Divide weight by height squared: 70 ÷ 3.0625 = 22.86
  4. Result: BMI = 22.9 (Normal weight)

❓ Frequently Asked Questions
What is a healthy BMI for adults?

A healthy BMI range is 18.5 to 24.9 according to the World Health Organization. For Asian populations, the healthy range is narrower: 18.5 to 22.9.

Is BMI accurate for athletes and bodybuilders?

No. BMI does not distinguish between muscle and fat. A muscular athlete with low body fat may have a high BMI and be classified as overweight despite being very healthy. Use body fat percentage instead.

Does BMI change with age?

The BMI formula itself doesn't account for age. However, body composition changes with age — older adults tend to have more body fat and less muscle. Some experts suggest slightly higher BMI ranges (25-27) may be acceptable for people over 65.

Is BMI different for men and women?

The BMI formula and categories are the same for both genders. However, women naturally have a higher body fat percentage than men (20-25% vs 15-20% for healthy adults).

Can children use this BMI calculator?

Standard BMI categories apply to adults 20 and older. For children and teens (ages 2-19), BMI is interpreted using age-specific and sex-specific percentiles, which requires a different calculator.

What should I do if my BMI is too high?

Consult your doctor first. A combination of balanced diet, regular exercise (150+ min/week), adequate sleep, and stress management is recommended. Use our Calorie Calculator and TDEE Calculator to plan your approach.

Who invented BMI?

BMI was developed by Belgian mathematician Adolphe Quetelet between 1830-1850. The term "Body Mass Index" was coined by Ancel Keys in 1972. Despite being nearly 200 years old, it remains the most widely used health screening metric.
