❓ Frequently Asked Questions
Should I choose a shorter or longer loan tenure?

A shorter tenure means higher EMIs but significantly less total interest. A longer tenure lowers your monthly burden but costs more in total. Rule of thumb: choose the shortest tenure where EMI stays below 40% of your income.

What is a good interest rate for a home loan in 2026?

Currently, competitive home loan rates in India range from 8.25% to 9.5%. If your rate is above 9%, consider balance transfer to a lower-rate lender.

How does a home loan balance transfer work?

You transfer your outstanding loan to a new bank offering a lower rate. The new bank pays off your old loan. Cost: 0.5-1% processing fee + ₹5-10K legal charges. Usually worth it if the rate difference is 0.5%+ and remaining tenure is 10+ years.

Can I get tax benefits on a home loan?

Yes. Under Section 24, you can deduct up to ₹2L/year on home loan interest. Under Section 80C, up to ₹1.5L on principal repayment. First-time buyers get additional ₹50K deduction under Section 80EEA.
