❓ Frequently Asked Questions
What is an RD (Recurring Deposit)?

An RD is a savings instrument where you deposit a fixed amount every month for a predetermined period. Interest is compounded quarterly in most banks. It's ideal for people who want to save systematically with guaranteed returns.

RD vs SIP — which is better?

RD offers guaranteed but lower returns (6-7%). SIP offers higher potential returns (12-15%) but with market risk. Choose RD for capital safety and SIP for wealth creation. Many financial advisors recommend doing both.

Can I change my RD monthly amount?

No. Once an RD is opened, the monthly amount is fixed. If you miss a payment, a small penalty is charged. You'd need to close the existing RD and open a new one for a different amount.
