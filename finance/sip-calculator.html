📈 How SIP Works — The Power of Compounding

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) is a disciplined method of investing a fixed amount in mutual funds at regular intervals. The key advantage is rupee cost averaging — you buy more units when prices are low and fewer when prices are high.

FV = P × [{(1+r)n − 1} ÷ r] × (1+r)

Where: P = Monthly SIP amount, r = Monthly rate of return, n = Total months

❓ Frequently Asked Questions
What is a step-up SIP?

A step-up SIP increases your monthly investment by a fixed percentage each year (typically 10%). This accounts for salary growth and dramatically increases long-term wealth.

What returns can I expect from mutual funds?

Historically, equity mutual funds in India have delivered 12-15% CAGR over 10+ year periods. Debt funds typically return 6-8%. Past performance does not guarantee future returns.

Can I stop my SIP anytime?

Yes. SIPs have no lock-in period (except ELSS funds which have a 3-year lock-in). You can pause, modify, or stop your SIP at any time through your mutual fund app or AMC website.

Is SIP tax-free?

SIP in ELSS funds qualifies for Section 80C deduction (up to ₹1.5L/year). For equity funds, LTCG above ₹1.25L is taxed at 12.5%. STCG is taxed at 20%. Debt fund gains are taxed at your income slab rate.

How much SIP do I need for ₹1 crore?

At 12% annual returns: ₹5,000/month for 25 years, or ₹10,000/month for 20 years, or ₹26,000/month for 15 years will grow to approximately ₹1 Crore.
